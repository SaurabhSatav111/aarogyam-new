NICU & PICU

Combined neonatal and paediatric intensive care with 24/7 specialist monitoring for critically ill newborns and children.

Book Appointment Call Now

About This Service

Our NICU provides specialised care for premature and critically ill newborns, while our PICU, led by Dr. Avinash Shelke, cares for critically ill children requiring intensive monitoring and support. Equipped with state-of-the-art life support systems, warmers, and phototherapy equipment, our intensive care units combine high-tech intervention with absolute care and attention for the most vulnerable young patients.

Who It's For

Premature or critically ill newborns (NICU) and critically ill children requiring intensive care (PICU).

What to Expect

Continuous monitoring, incubator or ventilator support if needed, and a dedicated specialist team available around the clock.

How It Works

1

Admission & Stabilisation

Immediate assessment and stabilisation upon admission.

2

Intensive Monitoring

24/7 vital tracking with neonatologist or paediatric intensivist oversight.

3

Recovery & Discharge Planning

Gradual step-down care with full guidance for parents before discharge.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between NICU and PICU?

NICU cares for newborns, PICU cares for older infants and children — both provide intensive, specialist-led care.

Can family members visit the ICU?

Yes, with specific visiting hours to ensure patient rest and infection control.

Who leads the PICU team?

Dr. Avinash Shelke, our Paediatric Intensivist with fellowship training in pediatric intensive care.
